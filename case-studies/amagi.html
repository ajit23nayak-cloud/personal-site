B2B · Enterprise Media-tech · 2024

Growth strategy for Amagi — the next $100M, built bottom-up from four user-journey levers.

Amagi is a global cloud broadcast + ad-tech platform — ~$100M ARR, 400+ TV networks, 150 countries. The question: how do you chart the next growth stage without diluting enterprise focus?

SectorB2B Media-tech · Cloud Broadcast + Ad-tech Stage~$100M ARR · 300 clients · 150 countries FrameAcquisition → Onboarding → Engagement → Monetization

Where Amagi sits today

  • Revenue: FY24 ~₹750 Cr ARR. Breakeven expected.
  • Mix: US 78% · Europe 15% · APAC 7%.
  • Clients: ~300, incl. NBCUniversal, WBD, A+E, Roku, Vice.
  • LTV : CAC between 4:1 and 6:1 — healthy, but acquisition is event-heavy (NAB, IBC, FAST Conference) and website conversion is soft.

North-star & growth equation

Number of Clients × ARPU/ACV. Both vectors — client count and average contract value — need to compound. Leaning on only one creates either a concentration problem or a stagnation problem.

Market size

  • TAM: ~$850 Bn (broadcast tech + OTT + TV & video ad market).
  • SAM: ~$112 Bn (after cloud-vs-on-prem share + reach constraints).
  • SOM: $8–11 Bn (realistic capturable share given competition).

Growth drivers: cloud playout penetration is still only ~8% in the US and ~1% in emerging markets. FAST is the single biggest compounding tailwind in CTV.

Lever 1 — Acquisition

What's working & what isn't

Direct + organic search dominate traffic — brand is strong in the existing TAM. But website engagement is soft: high bounce, ~1.5 minute average session. Synamedia and similar competitors pull longer sessions with more product-anchored content.

Prioritized channels

  1. Product integrations & partnerships. CDNs (Akamai, Cloudflare), hyperscalers (AWS, GCP), ad-tech. Each integration acts as a distribution wedge.
  2. Events & community. NAB, IBC, and a flagship FAST Conference that Amagi owns — not sponsors.
  3. Targeted content hubs. FAST monetization playbooks, CTV measurement primers. Fix the bounce-rate problem at source.

Worked example — Amagi × Akamai

A partnership thesis executed end-to-end: pain-point → partner fit → outreach script → first-call design → internal buy-in → GTM plan. The pain: content-delivery speed + scalability. The partner: Akamai. The approach:

  • Partnership test: Do we have PMF? Yes. Is there a demand gap? Yes. Would customers request this integration? Confirmed via ticket volume.
  • Outreach design: Targeted POCs on LinkedIn — EVP Global Sales, VP Media, Directors of BD & Strategic Partnerships. Functional + emotional + social framing in the follow-up.
  • GTM: Co-branded content, joint webinars, embedded case studies at mutual customers. Success metrics on lead gen, conversion, and NPS uplift.

Levers 2–4 — Engagement, Onboarding, Monetization

The frame continues across the other three levers — each with its own diagnosis, prioritization matrix, and experiments. The spine of the argument: when every lever is grounded in the ICP's JTBD, acquisition cost and expansion revenue move together, not in tension.

Takeaway

Most enterprise scale-ups at Amagi's stage default to "add salespeople, add regions, add logos." The more durable play is a bottom-up growth model where partnerships, integrations, and co-marketing compound — and where the website becomes a real funnel, not a brochure.
