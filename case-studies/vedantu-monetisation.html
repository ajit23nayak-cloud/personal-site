Pricing for a purchase that gets argued over the dinner table — System 2, done right.

Vedantu's hero product is a ₹80k, once-a-year, multi-stakeholder purchase. The decision involves a student, a parent, and often an influencer (uncle, senior, friend). Pricing can't be a button — it has to be a conversation.

Does Vedantu even pass the monetisation litmus test?

Yes — a flat ~20% retention curve (core + power users are >50% of the base), medium-to-high willingness to pay (especially on competitive-exam test prep), and a clear delta-4 value over the self-study alternative.

Vedantu's ASP sits inside the tuition/self-study band but delivers effectiveness closer to offline coaching. That's the arbitrage — and the story.

Who to charge (and who not to)

Segment by frequency × recency × spend × feature usage:

  • P0 — Champions, Loyal, Potential Loyalists: 4–6+ sessions / week, ₹80k–₹100k+ ARPU. Primary monetisation target.
  • P1 — New Customers, Need Attention: Focus is activation / engagement, not monetisation pressure.
  • P2 — Promising, About-to-sleep, Can't-lose-them: Retention + resurrection.
  • Skip — At Risk, Hibernating: Don't waste CRM bandwidth. Resurrect via a free offer, or let go.

When to charge — the perceived-value inflection

Today, a student exiting a free trigonometry masterclass gets hit with a 1-year course pitch on the next screen. No context transfer. No paywall that limits free value.

Pricing must appear right after the AHA moment — not before it. Free features should be limited either by count (3 masterclasses, then paywall) or by frequency (1 doubt per day), forcing the value discovery before the price ask.

What to charge for

  • Access — LIVE classes, post-class resources, assignments.
  • Output — backed by a marks-improvement promise (social proof + skin in the game).
  • Time — unlimited doubt-solving, recordings, mock tests.

How much to charge

Vedantu's ~₹80k/yr ASP against an offline-coaching substitute at ~₹1,50,000/yr leaves headroom. Three plays to widen the price–value gap:

  • Louder communication of outcomes — ranks, improvement, testimonials.
  • Add-ons like hybrid centers for face-to-face doubt-solving.
  • Stronger post-class material — better notes, better question banks.

How to show the price — cognitive-bias audit

Hits

  • Scratched-out MRP + net price (anchoring via discount).
  • One variant highlighted.
  • 15-day trial baked in.

Misses

  • No decoy. Two variants with a ~45% delta, purely on "personal teacher," is a hard jump. A third middle variant fixes the anchoring.
  • No feature-specific pricing. Pro Classic as a base + add-ons for doubts / mock tests lets users self-select.
  • No social proof on the pricing page. "Most bought by top 1000 rankers" is a one-line trust builder that nobody is using.

System 1 or System 2?

Is this a quick buy? No. Is it high-consideration, once-a-year, high-AOV, multi-stakeholder? Yes. That puts Vedantu firmly in System 2 pricing — slow, reasoned, iterative.

Which means the pricing page shouldn't be a checkout — it should be a comparison. Plan comparison, teacher profiles, sample classes, call-to-talk-to-an-advisor. Every one of those reduces purchase anxiety. Every one is low cost to build. Every one compounds.

Takeaway

For consumer products that sit at the intersection of high price, high emotion, and multiple stakeholders, pricing is a user-research problem disguised as a commercial one. Get the when, who, and how right; the how much almost takes care of itself.
