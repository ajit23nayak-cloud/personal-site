What I believe

Four convictions — distilled from twelve years of consulting, operating, and rebuilding growth engines. They're not posters. They're how I actually decide.

Strategy must touch the ground.

A strategy that doesn't translate into operating cadence, unit economics, and a weekly scoreboard is theatre. My bias is plans that ship — and systems that make shipping predictable.

Inner OS precedes outer OS.

The leader's clarity caps the org's clarity. Mental models, yoga, and quiet practice aren't adjacent to the work — they are the substrate on which good decisions compound.

Resets beat rewrites.

Most businesses don't need reinvention. They need honest diagnosis, fewer bets, and the courage to re-architect revenue before revenue forces the issue.

AI is leverage, not theatre.

The win isn't an "AI strategy deck." It's workflow automation, decision velocity, and analytics that move KPIs inside quarters — not keynotes.

02

Working with me

If we're considering working together — as colleagues, founders, or partners — here's the honest shape of it. Written plainly so there are no surprises later.

My value system

  • Clarity over comfort. I will ask the uncomfortable question in the room. Ambiguity is the tax; clarity is the compounding asset.
  • Ownership, not optics. I measure myself on outcomes shipped, not decks authored. Credit is cheap; consequence is not.
  • Long game always. I'd rather build a durable engine over three quarters than a viral win in three weeks.
  • Low agency is a disease. The person who moves first — with imperfect information — almost always wins. I hire, partner, and invest on this trait.
  • Humility in method, conviction in direction. Frameworks are tools, not gods. But once a direction is chosen, execution is non-negotiable.

How I operate

  • First 30 days, I listen. Diagnosis before prescription. No reorgs, no framework-dumping, no hero moves.
  • Weekly cadence is sacred. OKRs, 1:1s, business reviews — set up once, held religiously. Rhythm beats heroics.
  • Write, don't present. I prefer a one-pager over a 30-slide deck. If it can't survive a doc, it won't survive a quarter.
  • Bullet points over paragraphs. I think faster in structured bullets. Expect them in every doc I write.
  • I disagree, then commit. Argue hard in the room; leave aligned. Lobbying in the hallway is a tax on everyone.
  • Numbers end arguments. If a decision is stuck, the fastest path is usually a small spreadsheet — not another meeting.
  • Protect the calm. No messages after 9pm, no calls on Sunday unless the building is on fire. Sustainable pace is the only pace that compounds.
