Edtech · Acquisition

Pushing incremental CAC toward zero — via referral redesign and a content loop built on social proof.

Vedantu is a 10–15M MAU edtech with ~20M top-of-funnel. SEO was saturated. Paid had diminishing returns. The question: where is the next 10–15% of acquisition hiding, at near-zero CAC?

SectorK-12 Edtech FocusReferral + organic content loop

Why referral was under-earning

Vedantu already had a referral structure producing 10–15% of revenues. But it was loose:

  • Wrong audience. Every user — free or paid — saw a referral banner on the homepage. Half had never experienced an AHA moment.
  • Hidden discovery. The referral page was buried inside My Profile.
  • Cash only as currency. 15% cashback post-renewal. Renewals are 6–12 months out, so the reward was delayed past the trigger.

Referral, redesigned

Who — only AHA'd users

The earliest validated AHA signal at Vedantu is 5-star teacher + course rating, usually reached within 15–30 days of paid usage with 100% attendance. No asks before that.

How — contextual discovery

Move the referral prompt out of the profile page and into the moments that matter: post class-rating, post mock-test success, post grade improvement in a PTM.

Why — expand platform currencies

  • Money (continue cashback).
  • Access — 1:1 with teachers, topper learning strategies, curated book library.
  • Dopamine — if the referred student cracks JEE/NEET, the referrer's full fee is refunded retrospectively.

The parent variant — ICP-3's protective parents

For the "IB-school, double-income, parent-driven" ICP, referrals don't flow through the student — they flow through the parent. Triggered at the PTM when the parent's AHA moment lands (a templatized score-improvement card). Distributed through WhatsApp / Telegram community groups at work and in society. Social capital beats cashback here.

The content loop

The hook is simple: a JEE/NEET mock-test score is the single most powerful social-signaling asset in an Indian teenager's life.

After every Vedantu mock test, auto-generate a shareable reel:

  1. Screen 1: Predicted rank based on mock performance (predictive algorithm, not the test score itself).
  2. Screen 2: Which colleges that rank unlocks — with the student's photo Photoshopped onto the IIT Bombay / Delhi gate.
  3. Screen 3: "Share this with one friend who should also know."

Loop structure:

  • Hook: Reel of predictive rank + potential college admits.
  • Generator: Vedantu, auto-triggered after every mock test.
  • Distributor: Student via WhatsApp, Instagram, Telegram, Facebook.

Why this works

Three psychological levers stacked in one asset: dopamine (rank fantasy), social signal (photo at IIT gate), FOMO (your friends will see it). Generation cost is near-zero after the first engineering pass. Distribution cost is zero — the user does it.

Takeaway

Most acquisition teams chase new channels. The higher-leverage play at a mature platform is to re-architect the channels you already have — especially referrals — around the right moment, the right audience, and the right currency.
